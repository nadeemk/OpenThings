Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2, 2026

OpenThings is a to-do and planning app. This policy describes what data the app handles and what happens to it. The short version: your data stays on your device unless you create a sync account, we collect nothing else, and there are no ads or trackers.

Data stored on your device

Your to-dos, projects, areas, tags, checklists, notes, and settings are stored in a local database on your device. If you never create an account, this data never leaves your device.

Data synced to your account (optional)

If you create a sync account, the following is stored on our servers so your devices can stay in sync:

  • Your email address (for signing in)
  • Your to-do content (titles, notes, dates, tags, checklists, projects, and areas)

Synced data is transmitted over HTTPS and protected by row-level security: only your authenticated account can read or write your rows. We do not sell, share, or mine your data.

Calendar access (optional)

If you grant calendar permission, the app reads your device calendar to display events alongside your to-dos. Calendar data is displayed locally and is never uploaded or synced.

Notifications

Reminders are scheduled as local notifications on your device.

Analytics and tracking

The app contains no advertising, no third-party analytics, and no tracking SDKs.

Deleting your data

You can permanently delete your account and all synced data at any time from inside the app (sidebar → sync status → Delete account). Local data is deleted when you uninstall the app or clear its storage.
